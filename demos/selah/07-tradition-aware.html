Your question

"What do different Christian traditions believe about predestination and free will?"

Berean · Tradition-aware response

🤝 Shared across traditions

All major Christian traditions affirm that God is sovereign, that human beings bear moral responsibility, and that salvation is ultimately God's gift — not earned by human effort alone. The diversity emerges in how these truths are held together.

How different traditions approach this

Reformed

God's sovereign election is unconditional. Faith itself is a gift given to the elect (TULIP). Human will is free to choose, but always within the bounds of one's nature.

Catholic

Grace and free will cooperate. God's foreknowledge does not compel human choice. Predestination is understood in light of God's knowledge of human response (Molinism, Thomism debate).

Orthodox

Emphasizes synergy — human and divine cooperation. God wills all to be saved; human freedom is real and essential. Predestination is God's foreknowledge, not fatalistic decree.

Wesleyan

Prevenient grace enables genuine free will for all. God foreknows but does not foreordain specific individuals for damnation. Universal atonement; resistible grace.

Pentecostal

Broadly Wesleyan-Arminian. Emphasizes the Spirit's dynamic, universal work. Salvation is available to all who respond. Election is corporate (in Christ) rather than individual.

Anabaptist

Discipleship and community witness center stage. Doctrine takes a back seat to lived response. Salvation understood as ongoing, communal transformation more than a juridical transaction.
