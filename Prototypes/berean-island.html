"I am the vine; you are the branches. If you remain in me and I in you, you will bear much fruit; apart from me you can do nothing."
John 15:5 · BSB ↕ Compare
●
Answer
The vine metaphor emphasizes complete dependence on Christ. In first-century viticulture, branches that disconnected withered within hours — John uses this urgency to contrast abundant life with spiritual sterility.
