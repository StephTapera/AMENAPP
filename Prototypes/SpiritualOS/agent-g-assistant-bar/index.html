What does Romans 8:28 mean in context?
↑
"What does Romans 8:28 mean in context?"
✦
Berean
In Romans 8:28, Paul assures believers that God is sovereignly working through all circumstances — both joyful and painful — for the good of those who love Him and are called according to His purpose.

The Greek word for "works together" (synergei) suggests active, ongoing cooperation across all of life's events. Paul is not promising that all things are good, but that God is actively weaving them toward a redemptive end.

This verse sits at the heart of Paul's argument in Romans 8 — that nothing in creation can ultimately separate us from God's purpose for those He has called.
