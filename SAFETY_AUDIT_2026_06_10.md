## Executive Summary

This audit adds **9 CRITICAL**, **23 HIGH**, **22 MEDIUM**, and **14 LOW** new findings on top of the 2026-06-02 report. Several findings from the previous report are now **FIXED** by the Wave-1 P0 program (P0-1 through P0-7, P0-9/10 — see overlap dedup section below); this document tracks only the **net-new gaps** and the **remaining open items** from the previous report not yet covered.

The three most severe domains are:

1. **NCMEC mandatory reporting is legally non-compliant.** `ncmecReporter.js` queues records locally but never posts to the NCMEC CyberTipline HTTP API. NCMEC legal-hold Firestore collections have no explicit immutability rules. The CSAM content-hiding writes in `AmenChildSafetyService.swift` use `try?` (silent failure) — in a CSAM scenario a failed soft-delete leaves the content visible with no alert. These are federal legal exposure items under 18 U.S.C. § 2258A.

2. **Minor protection has multiple independent bypass paths.** Age verification is entirely client-side and self-reported. The `AgeCategory` iOS enum silently defaults to `.adult` for the canonical CF-set tier strings `tierB`/`tierC`/`blocked` (rawValue mismatch). Minors appear in the Algolia search index with no age-based filtering. The guardian email verification Cloud Function referenced in code comments does not exist. The `isGuardianApprovedContact()` default is permissive (allow) when no guardian document exists.

3. **The storage rules configuration is in a split-brain state.** `firebase.json` references `AMENAPP/storage.rules` which does not exist on disk. Future `firebase deploy --only storage` will silently fail or deploy the wrong file. The currently-live storage rules are unknown.

**This app is NOT ready for public launch.** The NCMEC pipeline gap and Algolia minor-discovery gap are federal legal exposure. Phases 1–2 below (CRITICAL and HIGH) must be fully resolved and legally reviewed before any minor-facing beta or App Store release.
